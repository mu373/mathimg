Managing equations in your project

A MathEdit project keeps all related equations in one place. Instead of scattered files, you have a single document containing every equation for a presentation, paper, or document. When you need to update equations, open the project, make changes, and re-export.

Organize by project

Create a separate MathEdit document for each project:

  • A presentation about machine learning
  • A research paper on differential equations
  • A textbook chapter on calculus

All equations stay together. When you revise your presentation, open the MathEdit project, update what you need, and export again.

Adding equations

Equations are separated by --- in your document. To add a new equation, type --- on its own line and write your LaTeX below it:

E = mc^2
\label{energy}

---

F = ma
\label{newton}

Labeling equations

Use \label{name} to give each equation a descriptive identifier:

\sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}
\label{sum-formula}

Labels appear in the sidebar and are used as filenames when exporting. Use descriptive names like quadratic-formula or loss-function.

If you don't add a label, MathEdit automatically assigns one (eq1, eq2, etc.).

Select an equation

Click on an equation in the sidebar or preview pane to select it. The editor jumps to its location in the document.

Delete an equation

To delete an equation, remove its content and the surrounding --- separators from the document.

Frontmatter for shared settings

Add a frontmatter section at the top of your document to define color presets and default settings that apply to all equations:

define.myblue: #0066ff
color: black

---

E = \color{myblue} mc^2

See Document syntax for details on frontmatter options.
